Biography

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Data Science at the University of North Texas. My research focuses on computer vision and machine learning, specifically aiming to enhance the safety, interpretability, and robustness of computer vision systems. I completed my PhD in 2024 at the University of Maryland, Baltimore County.

I am seeking highly motivated PhD students to join my lab in Fall 2025. As a professor leading cutting-edge research in Privacy-Enhanced Machine Learning, Federated Learning, Large Language Modeling, Visual Question Answering, Data Mining, Health Informatics, and Cybersecurity, I am committed to tackling complex challenges at the intersection of AI, privacy, and security. My lab focuses on interdisciplinary projects that aim to make a real-world impact.

Current Research Areas

Privacy Enhanced Machine Learning

Federated Learning
Training models across decentralized devices without sharing raw data.

Differential Privacy
Adding noise to data to prevent leakage of individual information.

Homomorphic Encryption
Performing computations on encrypted data without decryption.

Federated Learning

Privacy Preservation
Data remains on local devices, reducing exposure risks.

Communication Efficiency
Techniques like model aggregation and compression minimize data transfer overhead.

Heterogeneous Data Handling
FL accounts for non-IID (independent and identically distributed) data across clients.

Large Language Modeling

Privacy-Preserving LLMs
Integrating techniques like differential privacy and federated learning to secure user data.

Efficient Training & Inference
Optimizing models for reduced computational costs via distillation, quantization, and pruning.

Adaptation to Low-Resource Settings
Enhancing LLMs for languages and domains with limited training data.

Data Mining

Scalable Data Mining Algorithms for Big Data
Investigate efficient algorithms that can process massive datasets in real-time or near real-time, focusing on scalability and computational efficiency.

Explainable Data Mining Models
Create interpretable models that provide clear insights into the mining process, making results more transparent and actionable, especially in critical fields like healthcare or finance.

Health Informatics

Predictive Analytics in Healthcare
Use data mining and machine learning to predict patient outcomes, such as disease progression, treatment responses, and readmission risks.

Clinical Decision Support Systems (CDSS)
Design and evaluate intelligent systems that assist healthcare providers in making clinical decisions using patient data.

Cybersecurity

Adversarial Machine Learning and Robustness
Investigate the vulnerability of machine learning models to adversarial attacks, where small, carefully crafted perturbations in data can lead to incorrect predictions.

Cyber Threat Intelligence and Predictive Analytics
Use data mining, machine learning, and big data techniques to predict and identify emerging cybersecurity threats and attack patterns.
